## 1. Core Identity

`UNBOUND` is a target-zone displacement trump.

It does not merely destroy.
It does not merely exchange.
It dissolves one current fate-line
and takes its place.

Short formula:

```text
dissolve one target card
this trump takes that exact slot
```

---

## 2. Reading

`☷ DISSOLVE` here is not broad field removal.

It means the current fate-line is dissolved
out of its binding position.
Not all trumps — one. Chosen.

`☴ OBSERVE` here is not private inspection.

It means one target-line is singled out
from the currently compiled future.
The eye chooses which binding to cut.

So this trump reads as:

```text
one fate-line is unbound
another takes its place
```

This is not:

```text
global destruction
```

And not:

```text
random replacement
```

It is:

```text
chosen dissolution of a specific bound fate
```

---

## 3. Core Law

When `UNBOUND` becomes known:

1. Choose any card in `target zone`.
2. Dissolve that card from its current target slot.
3. Place `UNBOUND` into that exact slot.

Then route the dissolved card by class:

- if the dissolved card is a trump, move it to `trump zone`
- otherwise, move it to `grave`

Short formula:

```text
target card dissolved
UNBOUND takes its slot
dissolved trump -> trump zone
dissolved non-trump -> grave
```

---

## 4. Target Scope

`UNBOUND` acts on any card currently occupying a target slot.

That includes:

- trump cards
- non-trump cards
- not-revealed target cards
- shown target cards

This trump does not need extra visibility wording.
Its legal scope is defined by the zone itself.

Short formula:

```text
UNBOUND acts on target occupancy
not on visibility class
```

---

## 5. Exact Slot Consequence

`UNBOUND` does not enter target zone freely.

It must inherit the exact slot
of the dissolved target card.

This is important.

It means the card is not generic target gain.

It is:

```text
slot replacement
```

not:

```text
free target insertion
```

---

## 6. Why This Works

`UNBOUND` acts on the victory compiler directly.

It does not rewrite the whole target structure.
It dissolves one bound target position
and installs a different one in its place.

That makes it:

- precise
- disruptive
- highly contextual
- stronger when the compiler already holds a meaningful card
- less global than `RECAST`, but more focused
- less explosive than `RESET`, but more surgical

It also differs from `SWAP`:

- `SWAP` changes positions of already present trumps
- `UNBOUND` dissolves one compiled line and installs a different one

---

## 7. Routing Logic

The dissolved card should not re-resolve as an event.

If it is a trump,
it becomes chamber pressure:

```text
dissolved trump -> trump zone
```

If it is not a trump,
it becomes residue:

```text
dissolved non-trump -> grave
```

This keeps the event clean
and avoids accidental extra trump-trigger noise.

---

## 8. Relation to Trump Ecology

`UNBOUND` does not invent a separate ecology.

It uses:

- ordinary target-zone access rules
- ordinary class routing after dissolution
- ordinary trump-zone law for any dissolved trump
- ordinary grave law for any dissolved non-trump
- ordinary chain close handling

Its specificity is not in ecology.
Its specificity is in replacing a fate-line
inside the victory compiler itself.

---

## 9. Restrictions

`UNBOUND` does not:

- create a free new target slot
- dissolve multiple target slots at once
- re-trigger the dissolved target card
- bypass normal class routing
- affect ordinary zones by default

It is local to:

```text
target zone
```

---

## 10. Design Character

`UNBOUND` should feel:

- surgical
- decisive
- inevitable
- like a choice between fates
- less global than `RECAST`
- less violent than `RESET`
- more precise than `SWAP`
- more like cutting one thread than tearing the fabric

This is intentional.

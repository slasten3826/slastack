## 1. Core Identity

`HALT` is not a value trump.

It is a chain-boundary trump.

It does not cancel what has already begun.
It prevents further trump escalation from starting
after the current resolving item finishes.

Short formula:

```text
finish current resolution
deny further trump entry
```

## 3. HALT Law

If `HALT` is revealed during an ongoing resolution chain:

1. The current resolving item completes normally.
2. No further trump resolutions may begin during that same chain.
3. Any trump revealed later in that same chain becomes a `halted trump`.
4. Halted trumps do not resolve.
5. When the halted chain closes, all non-`HALT` trumps from that halted chain are shuffled into deck.
6. `HALT` itself enters ordinary trump ecology after the halted chain closes.

If `HALT` is revealed and there is no further pending trump resolution,
it has no additional chain effect.

Short formula:

```text
HALT resolves
current item finishes
later trumps become halted trumps
halted chain non-HALT trumps -> deck
HALT itself -> trump zone
```

## 4. Halted Trumps

`HALTED TRUMPS` is a rules term.

It means:

```text
trumps revealed after HALT in the same halted chain,
but not allowed to begin resolution
```

Important:

- `HALT` itself is not a halted trump
- a halted trump is revealed, but unresolved
- halted trumps do not enter ordinary trump zone
- halted trumps do not go to grave
- halted trumps are flushed directly back into deck after the halted chain ends

This bypass exists because `HALT` is meant to stop overflow,
not to overload `trump zone` with the very cascade it is suppressing.

Important extension:

all other non-`HALT` trumps that already participated in that halted chain
also follow the same deck-flush ending.

So a halted chain does not partly park into `trump zone`
while the rest is flushed.

It closes as one interrupted anomaly contour.

Canonical compression:

```text
HALTed chain parks nothing except HALT itself
```

## 5. Why This Works

Without a boundary trump,
late-game trump ecology can keep expanding into long resolution chains.

`HALT` introduces a structural answer:

```text
the chain may complete,
but it may not continue growing
```

This does not ban cascade globally.
It creates a rare counter-force inside the ecology.

It also prevents `HALT` from loading `trump zone`
with the same overflow it is supposed to stop.

`HALT` did not appear as an arbitrary effect idea.

It appeared as a pressure-release answer to trump flow that could otherwise
keep cycling, expanding, and threatening effective infinite-loop behavior.

So `HALT` should be read as:

```text
hard discharge of runaway trump tension
```

It is not the only future trump that may interact with that flow,
but it is the one that stops it decisively.

## 6. Important Clarification

`HALT` does not mean:

- cancel everything
- undo the board
- revert the current action
- stop resolution mid-instruction

It means:

- let the current resolving item finish
- block any later trump initiation in that same chain

## 7. Solo Behavior

If `HALT` appears alone,
with no later trump entry to suppress,
it does nothing beyond its own ordinary resolution.

This is intentional.

`HALT` is context-sensitive by design.

Its strength depends on whether there is further trump pressure trying to enter.

## 9. Edge Cases

### HALT revealed as the first and only trump

No additional effect.

### HALT revealed in the middle of a chain

The current resolving item completes.
Later revealed trumps in that chain become halted trumps.
They do not resolve.
When the halted chain closes,
all non-`HALT` trumps from that chain are shuffled into deck.
`HALT` itself enters ordinary trump ecology.

### HALT revealed after another trump already started resolving

That earlier resolving item finishes normally.
`HALT` only blocks trump starts after that point.

### Multiple trumps revealed after HALT

None of them resolve.
All of them become halted trumps.
All non-`HALT` trumps from that chain are shuffled into deck after the chain closes.

### HALT and ordinary non-trump steps

Normal remaining steps of the current resolving item may complete.
`HALT` is about preventing further trump initiation,
not tearing apart the current instruction body.
